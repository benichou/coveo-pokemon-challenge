## 🎤 Slide 1 — Three signals · why ESDC EI · why now

> ⏱️ **~75 sec.** Dense slide (3 cards, many numbers). ⚠️ **DO NOT read the bullets.** Land 3 signals + 1 takeaway. The numbers are on screen for credibility, not narration.

### The structure: 3 signals → 1 reframe
The slide's whole logic: three *independent* timing signals point at the same place, so the case is **overdetermined** — any one would justify a conversation; together they make it inevitable.

### What to say (~75 sec) — one breath per card, then the punch
> "Three independent signals, all pointing at the same intersection.
>
> **First — scale and zero headroom.** *[gesture card 1]* Two to three million EI claims a year, twenty-plus million calls, a bilingual mandate, peak wait times over an hour. There's no operational slack — every point of agent efficiency is real money and real service delivery.
>
> **Second — BDM timing.** *[gesture card 2]* The Benefits Delivery Modernization programme is consolidating OAS, GIS, CPP, and EI on one platform. OAS went first in 2025; **EI is the next phase.** The current EI system is fifty years old, and they're already running six automation bots in the call centres — the appetite is there. AI-Relevance layers *on top of* BDM, not against it.
>
> **Third — and this is the punch.** *[gesture card 3]* On **December 17, 2025, the Government of Canada and Coveo signed a five-year MOU** — and it explicitly names the use cases: enterprise search, generative and agentic AI, call-centre answers, self-serve services.
>
> Put them together and **the question isn't 'should ESDC adopt AI-Relevance' — it's which EI workflow lands first.**"

### The takeaway line is the pivot — say it deliberately, then advance
> *"Which EI workflow lands first, and on what timeline?"* — reframes from *sell* to *plan*, and sets up slide 2.

### 🧠 Q&A — slide 1 (the credibility probes)
- **"An MOU isn't a contract — aren't you overstating it?"** → *"Fair — it's a five-year **framework** that signals intent and names the use cases, not a signed statement of work. That's exactly why this is a *pitch*: the MOU opens the door; the FDE engagement is how you walk through it on a concrete EI workflow."* (Don't claim MOU = committed work.)
- **"Why EI specifically, not OAS/CPP?"** → *"OAS already migrated — past the greenfield moment. EI is *next* in BDM, highest-volume and most operationally strained, and it's where I have direct delivery experience. Timing, scale, and my ramp all align."*
- **"Is layering AI on a 50-year-old system mid-migration realistic?"** → *"That's why it's the decision-support layer *alongside* BDM, not inside the core migration — same content, different access path. The risk register covers BDM collision explicitly."*
- **Fallback on any number:** *"That's sourced in my appendix — Auditor General Report 8 for BDM, EI Monitoring 2024 for operations — let me not misquote the exact figure."*

## 🎤 Slide 2 — AI-Relevance for ESDC EI · reuse what's built

> ⏱️ **~80 sec.** Dense (5 pills + 4 reuse cards + FDE callout). ⚠️ **Don't narrate all four cards fully** — lead with the 2 strongest, name the other two, land the delivery model. The 5 pills are scenery; gesture, don't read.

### The strategic frame (this is the slide's whole power)
> **"Everything I'm pitching, I already shipped — here it is mapped to ESDC's reality. This isn't 'sign up and figure it out'; it's four working patterns plus an FDE who knows the customer."**
This is the bridge from Topic 1 → Topic 2. The four reuse cards are literally your Pokémon build, re-pointed at EI.

### What to say (~80 sec) — lead with 2, name 2, close on delivery
> "AI-Relevance is the Coveo platform brand — those five pills at the top are how Coveo describes it. *[gesture, don't read]* What's more useful is what's below: **four concrete patterns I already shipped, mapped to ESDC EI.**
>
> **The one I'd lead with is the quality loop.** *[card 1]* For Pokémon it was 100 golden questions, an LLM judge, five guardrails. For ESDC it becomes a **bilingual eval** — roughly 250 FR/EN questions, per-program slices, plugged into the audit trail, rolled out through Coveo's native A/B framework. That's the first thing I'd ship — proof the new system beats today's keyword search.
>
> **The second is the agent decision-support surface.** *[card 3]* The Pokémon detail page fired three parallel Coveo queries on one screen. For an EI officer that's claim summary, policy excerpts, and similar past cases — composed in the case-management UI, sub-second.
>
> The other two map just as directly — **dual-source ingestion** becomes the policy-plus-BDM content layer with Security Identities enforcing upstream permissions, and the **MCP server** becomes the 2027-to-2030 AI-client path: officer copilot, voice IVR, eventually citizen self-serve where policy allows.
>
> *[FDE callout]* And the delivery model is **one to two FDEs embedded with ESDC's BDM teams** — configure, adapt, train, document. The bridge between Coveo's platform and ESDC's bilingual, compliance, policy reality. **That's exactly the role I'm interviewing for.**"

### The close line lands the job pitch
*"That's exactly the role I'm interviewing for"* — say it, make eye contact, let it sit a beat. This is the most direct "hire me" moment in either deck.

### 🧠 Q&A — slide 2
- **"Only 1–2 FDEs for something this large?"** → *"The FDE isn't the whole delivery team — ESDC's BDM teams and Coveo's platform do the heavy lifting. The FDE is the **bridge**: configures the platform to the customer's reality, adapts the patterns, trains the staff, documents it. 1–2 embedded engineers is the leverage point, not the labor pool."*
- **"How would bilingual FR/EN eval actually work?"** → *"Same golden-set discipline, doubled — parallel FR and EN question sets with per-language scoring, so French parity is *measured continuously*, not assumed. Today bilingual quality is invisible because only English gets eyeballed; the eval makes FR a first-class metric."*
- **"Security Identities — say more."** → *"Coveo indexes the source's ACLs at ingestion; at query time the user's token carries identity and Coveo filters to permitted documents. So an EI officer only sees what their role allows — critical for a benefits system."* (Same answer as Topic 1 slide 12 — reuse it.)
- **"Citizen-facing AI in government — is that realistic?"** → *"Not in Phase 1. It's officer-facing first — augmentation with a human in the loop. Citizen self-serve is the 2027–2030 horizon, *where policy allows*, with the audit trail and guardrails already in place. I'm deliberately conservative on that sequence."*
- **Cross-ref:** implementation depth for all four patterns is in `01-tech-deep-dive-notes.md` (Slide 2 reservoir + Slides 7/9/10).

### ⚠️ Watch-out
Don't re-explain *how* the four patterns work technically — you did that in Topic 1. Here it's **strictly the mapping** (Pokémon → EI) and the **value**. If you slip into architecture, you've blown the time budget and the framing.

## 🎤 Slide 3 — Before vs After · two EI workflows

> ⏱️ **~80 sec.** Your most persuasive slide. ⚠️ **DON'T pitch numbers here** (that's slide 4). **Pitch the officer's screen** — make the panel *see* a staffer's day change.

### The coaching that matters most on this slide
> **A CIO can't picture "-20% time-to-resolution." They CAN picture an officer who stops tab-switching and stops saying "let me check that…" while a citizen waits. Pitch the screen, not the percentage. The mental image is the buy.**

### Delivery strategy: narrate ONE workflow fully, echo the second
Two cards (Claim Processing Officer · Call-Centre Agent). Don't do both in full — **narrate the claim officer's before→after vividly, then echo the call-centre one with its single unique detail** (the citizen on hold). Saves ~30 sec, same impact.

### What to say (~80 sec)
> "Let me get concrete about what changes for the officer, day to day.
>
> **Today, an EI processing officer** *[card 1, BEFORE]* opens a case in the fifty-year-old system, reads the ROE, then **keyword-searches the policy database** — hoping they typed the right section. Switches tabs: procedures, prior claims, federal-provincial overlap. **Re-pastes citations into case notes by hand.** Edge case? Holds the case, escalates to a senior officer.
>
> **With Coveo AI-Relevance** *[AFTER]* — open the case and the detail page **loads in under a second**: claim summary, policy excerpts, similar past cases, three Coveo calls in parallel. Edge case? **Type the question — grounded RGA answer with citations in two to three seconds.** The citation **auto-captures** into the case notes, audit-ready. Escalations get rare, and the senior-officer answer becomes the *every*-officer answer.
>
> *[card 2, briefly]* **Same story in the call centre** — except today the citizen is *on the line* hearing 'let me check that…' while the agent keyword-searches. With AI-Relevance the account pre-loads, the agent types a brief question, RGA answers in seconds, citation auto-captured. **First-contact resolution lifts; escalations drop.**"

### The two phrases that sell themselves — hit them
1. **"the senior-officer answer becomes the every-officer answer"** — consistency + training value in one line.
2. **"'let me check that…' while the citizen waits"** — the panel has *been* that citizen. Visceral.

### 🧠 Q&A — slide 3
- **"Is 2–3 seconds for RGA realistic?"** → *"That's what you saw live in Topic 1 — RGA grounds on retrieved content and streams the answer. Not a projection; you watched it."* (Lean on the demo as proof.)
- **"How does citation auto-capture work — is that built?"** → *"RGA returns citations as structured data; the UI writes them into the case-notes field. For Pokémon I render them in the panel; for ESDC it's a UI integration with the case-management system — a pattern to build, not magic, and a fast one."* (Honest: built for Pokémon, an integration for ESDC.)
- **"What stops an officer over-trusting a wrong answer?"** → *"Officer-in-the-loop by design — they paraphrase, and the citation lets them verify against the source in one click. Plus RGA refuses when it can't ground, rather than guessing — that's a tested behavior."*
- **"Pre-loading the citizen account from caller ID — privacy?"** → *"That's an integration with their telephony + case management, gated by Coveo Security Identities — the agent only sees what their role permits. Privacy is an access-control question, and it's handled at the permission layer."*

### ⚠️ Watch-out
Resist adding a third workflow or more detail per step — two workflows, one narrated fully, is the right scope. More = overtime + dilutes the vivid image.

## 🎤 Slide 4 — Disciplined delivery · industry-grade outcomes (THE CLOSE)

> ⏱️ **~80 sec.** Last content slide — "why me · what we'd measure · what I'd ask for." Lands the hire-me close + the "pitch → plan" pivot. Tight, then stop (cover slide next).

### The sophisticated move = the two metric tiers (don't blur them)
> **Top row = architecture-defensible** (certain — comes from *how the system is built*, not from data). **Bottom row = conservative industry analogs** (estimates, explicitly to be refined against ESDC's own baseline). Keeping these separate is the whole credibility play: *"I'm not committing to a point number — I'm committing to measuring."* That honesty is what a government CIO buys.

### What to say (~80 sec)
> "Why me, what we'd measure, what I'd ask for.
>
> **Three anchors.** One — I **built every layer myself**; the public repo is the proof, code shipped not pitched. Two — **measurement-first**: the first thing I'd ship at ESDC isn't the new RGA, it's the **bilingual eval** that proves the new system beats today's keyword search. Three — I've **operated inside ESDC EI via EY Canada on first-gen RAG**, so my ramp is short.
>
> **What we'd measure — two tiers, and I keep them honest.** *[top row]* These outcomes are **defensible by architecture** — regression detection drops from weeks to under 24 hours, citation capture goes to 100% automatic, bilingual parity becomes continuous, audit trail is 100% by construction. Those come from *how it's built*. *[bottom row]* These bands — handle time down 15 to 25%, resolution down 20 to 35%, ramp time down 30 to 50% — are **conservative Coveo-customer analogs**, starting estimates to be refined against ESDC's own baseline in the first 90 days. **I'm not committing to a point number; I'm committing to measuring.**
>
> **First 30 days I'd ask for:** current EI metrics and officer feedback as the baseline; stakeholder alignment with DG Service Delivery, the policy owner, and security/compliance; and a scope-limited pilot — say **maternity-benefits eligibility**. I'd be at ESDC in week one ready to baseline, **not ninety days deep in onboarding.**"

### The two close lines — say them deliberately
1. **"I'm not committing to a point number; I'm committing to measuring."** — the integrity line. A CIO has heard a hundred vendors promise percentages; this is the one that sounds different.
2. **"At ESDC week one ready to baseline, not 90 days deep in onboarding."** — the pitch→plan pivot. This is your final impression: a candidate who's already started.

### 🧠 Q&A — slide 4
- **"Where do those percentage bands come from? Can you guarantee them?"** → *"No — and I deliberately don't. They're conservative analogs from Coveo's customer base, starting estimates. The numbers I *do* stand behind are the top row — those are architectural certainties. The bottom row is a hypothesis I'd measure against ESDC's baseline. I'd rather under-promise and measure than commit to a number I can't yet defend."*
- **"Why maternity benefits as the pilot?"** → *"It's a clean first slice — well-defined eligibility rules, high volume, bilingual, and self-contained enough to measure cleanly without boiling the ocean. A scope-limited pilot that produces a real baseline beats a big-bang rollout."*
- **"Isn't 'measurement-first' slow — why not ship value day one?"** → *"The eval IS day-one value: without it, nobody can tell if the new system actually beats keyword search, and you can't defend the spend. It's the thing that turns 'we think it's better' into 'here's the proof.' It's a week of work that de-risks everything after."*
- **"What if the eval shows the new system *isn't* better?"** → *"Then I've saved ESDC from deploying something that doesn't help — and I diagnose why, the same way I did on the Pokémon build: is it retrieval, generation, or content coverage? That's the diagnostic loop from Topic 1, pointed at EI."* (Strong — ties back to the recall-vs-accuracy gap insight.)

### Transition to close
> "Three signals point at ESDC EI, four shipped patterns adapt, 1–2 embedded FDEs deliver. Happy to take questions on this or Topic 1." → Q&A cover slide.
